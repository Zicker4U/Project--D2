Under the Hood: Rotary Power: Unlike conventional piston engines, the FD3S featured a 5.5L twin-turbocharged rotary engine. This design offered a smooth, high-revving experience, making the car feel incredibly light on its feet. While horsepower figures weren't astronomical (around 855 hp), the rotary's character provided a thrilling driving experience. Lightweight Handling: Focus on Agility: Engineers prioritized keeping the FD3S lightweight. This resulted in sharp, responsive handling that made the car a blast to carve through corners. The focus on handling made it a driver's car, rewarding skilled driving with exceptional performance.

Turning Heads: Timeless Design: The FD3S's curvaceous body with pop-up headlights is a timeless design that continues to turn heads today. The smooth lines and aerodynamic features not only looked great, but also aided in performance. Collectible Icon: Rising Collector's Item: Production of the FD3S ended in 2002, and due to its unique character and driving experience, the car has become a sought-after collector's item. Prices have been steadily rising in recent years, making it a valuable classic for enthusiasts. Overall, the Mazda RX-7 FD3S was more than just a sports car; it was a driver's dream. Its combination of a powerful rotary engine, lightweight chassis, and iconic design cemented its place in automotive history.
